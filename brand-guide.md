| Role | Font | Notes |
|---|---|---|

| Body / UI | **Poppins** | Sans-serif. 14px base, weight 400. Clean and modern. |

| Element | Size |
|---|---|
| H1 | 48px |
| H2 | 44px |

**Key treatment:** Primary headings often use ALL CAPS with Playfair Display — gives a bold, established feel while staying elegant.

## Logo

- **Wordmark:** "MTN BUFF" — clean wordmark treatment
- **Versions:** White (for dark backgrounds), Dark (for light backgrounds)
- **Max height:** 40px at desktop; scale responsively
- **Don't:** Add effects, stretch, or place on busy backgrounds without contrast

## Design System

### Buttons
- Border radius: 5px (slightly rounded, not pill-shaped)
- Solid fill (primary orange `#b53c12`) with hover state shifting toward accent `#d47f2f`
- Text: white, Poppins, uppercase or sentence case

### Cards / Containers
- Drop shadow: `0px 20px 40px rgba(51,51,51,0.1)` — subtle, soft depth
- Clean white or off-white backgrounds

### Spacing
- Section padding (vertical): ~120px
- Section padding (horizontal): ~45px
- Icons: 20–32px, color shifts to primary orange on hover

### Imagery
- Feature photography with gradient overlays at a 214.67° angle
- Drone footage, 3D tours, professional photography are core to their brand
- Outdoor/mountain lifestyle imagery is on-brand
- Video testimonials are a major content format — social proof is visual

## Brand Voice & Tone

**Summary:** Professional enough to be credible, casual enough to be a real person. Confident without being arrogant. Relatable over corporate.

### Voice Characteristics
- **Conversational and direct** — talks to the reader like a trusted friend, not a salesperson
- **Slightly irreverent** — not afraid of a cheeky analogy ("Selling a home is a lot like online dating")
- **Reassuring under pressure** — acknowledges the stakes without creating fear
- **Locally grounded** — deep pride in Northern Utah, mountain culture, community
- **Education-first** — positions the team as translators of market complexity, not just door-openers

### Tone Examples (straight from their site)
> "This is quite frankly the largest transaction you will ever make. We don't take that lightly."

> "You wouldn't go out to family pictures without getting all dolled up would you?"

> "We have a good reputation in the area that will help your deal across the finish line."

> "If you aren't happy with us, we will let you out of your contract. Period. No strings attached."

### Language Patterns
- Action-oriented verbs: "doll up," "shine," "stand out," "eat, breathe, and sleep real estate"
- Strategic repetition for emphasis
- Direct second-person address ("you," "your")
- Data-backed claims without being dry — always grounded in local context
- Acknowledges difficulty and effort openly: "These are work but trust me they WORK!"

### What to Avoid
- Cold, corporate real estate speak
- Overly formal sentence structures
- Generic platitudes without specifics
- Fear-based or pressure-driven language

## Content Themes & Pillars

1. **Market education** — translating Utah real estate data for everyday buyers/sellers
2. **Regional expertise** — deep local knowledge of mountain communities (Ogden Valley, Wasatch Back, Morgan)
3. **Agent personality** — "behind the agent" storytelling; the humans behind the brand matter
4. **Strategic preparation** — helping clients act smart, not reactive
5. **Community integration** — local events, awareness, roots

**Primary content formats:** Blog posts, podcast episodes, video testimonials, market update reports

## Key Differentiators (for messaging assets)

1. **The Guarantee** — "If you aren't happy with us, we let you out of your contract. Period."
2. **Outdoor enthusiast identity** — they live the mountain lifestyle they're selling access to
3. **Full-service marketing** — photography, videography, 3D tours, drone footage, multi-platform digital presence
4. **Education over transaction** — they want clients informed, not dependent
5. **Local reputation** — "We have a good reputation in the area that will help your deal across the finish line"

## Asset Build Checklist (when creating branded materials)

- [ ] Use Playfair Display for headlines (ALL CAPS for primary headings)
- [ ] Use Poppins for body copy at 14px
- [ ] Primary color: `#b53c12` for CTAs and key elements
- [ ] Accent: `#d47f2f` for warmth and secondary elements
- [ ] Tone: conversational, direct, slightly irreverent — never stuffy
- [ ] Include local Utah/mountain references where natural
- [ ] Lead with education or value, not a hard sell
- [ ] If using imagery: warm, outdoor, mountain-lifestyle photography
- [ ] Reference their tagline when relevant: "Finding Your Shelter from Life's Storms"
