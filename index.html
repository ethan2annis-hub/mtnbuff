The 7 Levers

What You Can Ask
For Right Now

Each comes with real dollar values — because vague advice doesn't help you at the negotiating table.

01 01 /

The Rate Buydown

Ask the seller to pay cash at closing to permanently lower your interest rate — or to cover the first two years at a reduced rate (called a "2-1 buydown").

💰 Worth up to $400/month in year one on a $400K home. That's $4,800 you keep in year one alone.

In 2022: Sellers laughed at this. Now it's a standard ask.

Ask for this if…

You're stretching to afford the monthly payment. Even shaving half a point off your rate makes a real dent over 30 years — and the seller is motivated to close.

02 02 /

Closing Cost Credits

Ask the seller to cover some or all of your closing costs. They typically run 2–5% of the purchase price — money you'd otherwise bring out of pocket on top of your down payment.

💰 On a $400K home: up to $20,000 you don't have to bring to closing.

This is now one of the most common concessions in Northern Utah.

Ask for this if…

Your down payment is eating up most of your savings. Getting closing costs covered means you keep cash in your pocket for repairs, moving, or just breathing room after you move in.

03 03 /

Inspection Repairs & Credits

You can now inspect the home AND negotiate. After inspection, ask for a repair credit (cash at closing), require the seller to fix specific issues, or use findings to renegotiate the price.

💰 Inspection credits commonly run $2,000–$10,000+ depending on what's found.

In 2022: Buyers waived inspections entirely just to compete. That era is over.

Ask for this if…

The home is older or hasn't been updated in a while. Even a "clean" inspection on a 20-year-old house will surface deferred maintenance — and that's negotiating ammo.

04 04 /

Post-Appraisal Price Reduction

If the home appraises below your offer price, you can now negotiate the purchase price down to match — or walk away with your earnest money intact.

💰 In 2022, buyers routinely paid $20K–$50K over appraisal. You don't have to do that anymore.

Sellers understand this. Most would rather adjust than restart.

Ask for this if…

The home has been sitting a while or had a previous price drop. A seller who's already reduced once is far more likely to accept an appraised value than gamble on finding a better offer.

05 05 /

Appliances & Personal Property

Washers, dryers, refrigerators, outdoor furniture, window treatments — sellers will often throw these in now to sweeten a deal that's sitting.

💰 Replacing appliances yourself runs $3,000–$8,000 easily. Just ask.

In 2022: You were lucky if the seller left the toilet paper.

Ask for this if…

The home has been on the market 30+ days with no offers. A seller who's anxious to move on is usually happy to leave things behind rather than deal with hauling them out.

06 06 /

Flexible Closing Timeline

Need 60 days to close because you're selling your current home first? Or want to move fast before rates shift? You can now negotiate the closing date to work for your life, not the seller's convenience.

💰 Flexibility prevents costly bridge loans or double rent situations. The value is in what you avoid.

Timeline flexibility was essentially non-negotiable in a hot market. Not anymore.

Ask for this if…

You're selling your current home at the same time. A flexible close gives you a cushion so you're not paying two mortgages — or scrambling to find temporary housing between transactions.

07 07 /

The Home Warranty

Ask the seller to include a one-year home warranty covering major systems — HVAC, plumbing, electrical. If something breaks in year one, you're not paying out of pocket.

Small ask. Huge peace of mind.

Ask for this if…

You're a first-time buyer or purchasing an older home. When you're already stretched thin on a new purchase, an unexpected $4,000 HVAC repair in month three can really sting.

💰 Costs the seller $500–$700. Could save you $5,000+ if the furnace goes out in February.
