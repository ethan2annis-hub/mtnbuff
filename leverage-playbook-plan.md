## What We're Building (And Why It Works)

**The lead magnet:** A PDF guide called something like *"The Northern Utah Buyer's Leverage Playbook: What You Can Negotiate Right Now That You Couldn't in 2022"*

**The core idea:** The housing market in Northern Utah shifted in buyers' favor in 2024–2025. Most buyers don't know this yet — they're still mentally operating in the "panic buy, waive everything, overpay" mindset from the 2021–2022 frenzy. This guide tells them the truth: the leverage is back, here's exactly what you can ask for, here's what that's worth in real dollars.

**Why this works as a lead magnet:**
- It's timely (market just shifted — this is news)
- It's specific to their geography (Northern Utah, not generic)
- It delivers real financial value (knowing you can negotiate a 2-1 rate buydown could save you $6,000–$10,000+)
- It positions MTN BUFF as the local expert who actually understands the market
- The natural next step after reading it is: "I want one of these agents in my corner"

**Pages 4–10 — The 7 Things You Can Negotiate (The Meat)**

Each "thing" gets its own short section (roughly half a page each). Structure each one the same way:

> **What it is** — one sentence explanation
> **What it was like in 2022** — one sentence (sellers laughed at this)
> **What it's like now** — current reality
> **Real dollar value** — what this is actually worth to you financially
> **How to ask for it** — one or two sentences on how to bring it up with your agent

---

**The 7 Negotiation Levers:**

**#1 — The Seller-Paid Rate Buydown**
*What it is:* You ask the seller to pay cash upfront to lower your mortgage interest rate for 2 years (called a "2-1 buydown") or permanently.
*Real dollar value:* On a $400K home, a 2-1 buydown can save you ~$300–$400/month in year 1. That's $4,800 you kept.
*The ask:* "We'd like a seller concession of $X toward a permanent rate buydown."

**#2 — Closing Cost Credits**
*What it is:* Seller pays some or all of your closing costs at the table. Closing costs typically run 2–5% of the purchase price.
*Real dollar value:* On a $400K home, that's up to $20,000 you don't have to bring to closing.
*The ask:* "We're requesting $8,000 toward buyer closing costs."

**#3 — The Home Warranty**
*What it is:* A 1-year warranty covering major systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical) paid for by the seller. Typically $500–$700.
*Real dollar value:* If something breaks in year one, you're covered.
*In 2022:* Nobody asked for this. Sellers would have just moved on.

**#4 — Inspection Repairs or Credits**
*What it is:* After your inspection, you can now negotiate a repair credit (cash toward repairs at closing) or require the seller to fix things before closing.
*Real dollar value:* Inspection credits commonly run $2,000–$10,000+ depending on what's found.
*In 2022:* Buyers waived inspections entirely to win. Now? You can inspect AND negotiate.

**#5 — Extended Closing Timeline (Or Shorter)**
*What it is:* You can negotiate the timeline to work for YOU — longer closing if you need to sell something first, or shorter if you want to lock in before rates move.
*Real dollar value:* Flexibility has cash value. A longer closing window prevents you from needing bridge financing.

**#6 — Price Reductions After Appraisal**
*What it is:* If the home appraises below the offer price, you can now negotiate the purchase price down to match — or walk away with your earnest money.
*Real dollar value:* In 2022, buyers routinely paid $20K–$50K above appraisal. Now? The seller has to deal with reality.

**#7 — Furniture, Appliances, and Personal Property**
*What it is:* Washers, dryers, refrigerators, patio furniture, outdoor equipment, window treatments — sellers will now often include these to sweeten the deal.
*Real dollar value:* Replacing appliances on your own runs $3,000–$8,000 easily.
*In 2022:* You were lucky if the seller left the doorknobs.

## Step 2: Design the PDF

**Tools to use (in order of recommendation):**

1. **Canva** (free or Pro at $15/mo) — easiest, drag and drop, real estate templates available. Start here.
2. **Adobe InDesign** — if you want pixel-perfect control and already know it
3. **Google Slides** — works in a pinch, surprisingly good for guides

**Design rules using MTN BUFF brand:**
- Primary color `#b53c12` (burnt orange) — use for headers, section dividers, callout boxes
- Accent `#d47f2f` (terracotta) — use for highlights, icons, secondary accents
- Font: Playfair Display for section headers (ALL CAPS), Poppins for body text
- White/off-white backgrounds (`#fafafa`) for body sections
- Photography: warm, outdoor, mountain lifestyle or real Utah neighborhoods
- Keep it 12–14 pages max — people won't read a 30-page PDF

**What makes it feel premium:**
- Consistent margins (don't crowd the edges)
- One data visualization (the 2022 vs. 2026 comparison table)
- Pull quotes in callout boxes
- Icons next to each negotiation lever
- Real agent photo of Paden (or the team) — humanizes it

**Output format:** Export as PDF, optimized for web (not print). Keep file size under 5MB so it emails fast.

## Step 3: Create the Landing Page

This is the page where people trade their email for the guide. It needs to be simple — one job only: get the email.

**What the page needs:**

1. **Headline** — same as the guide title (or a variation)
2. **Subheadline** — 1–2 sentences on who this is for and what they'll get
3. **3–5 bullet points** — specific things they'll learn (use the negotiation levers)
4. **Email capture form** — first name + email only. Every extra field kills conversions.
5. **CTA button** — "Send Me the Guide" or "Get the Playbook Free"
6. **Small trust line** — "No spam. Just real market intel from agents who actually sell here."
7. *Optional:* A mockup image of the PDF cover (makes it feel real and valuable)

**Platform options:**
- **Carrd** (~$19/year) — dead simple, fast, great for single landing pages
- **Mailchimp landing pages** (free) — if they're already using Mailchimp
- **Their existing website** — if mtnbuff.com has a CMS (WordPress, Squarespace, etc.) they can add a page

**The URL:** Something clean like `mtnbuff.com/playbook` or `mtnbuff.com/guide`

# PART 3: TRACKING SUCCESS

## The Metrics That Matter (And What "Good" Looks Like)

Here's how to know if this is working. Think of it as a funnel — people drop off at each step, and you want to know where.

```
TRAFFIC → LANDING PAGE VIEWS
           ↓
        EMAIL OPT-INS (Landing Page Conversion Rate)
           ↓
        EMAIL OPEN RATES
           ↓
        LINK CLICKS (Back to site / book a call)
           ↓
        BOOKED CALLS / DIRECT INQUIRIES
           ↓
        CLOSED TRANSACTIONS
```

---

### Metric 1: Landing Page Conversion Rate
**What it is:** Of everyone who visits the landing page, what % gives you their email?
**How to track:** Your landing page tool (Carrd, Mailchimp, etc.) will show you visits vs. signups
**What's good:** 20–40% is solid for a targeted real estate lead magnet. Below 15% means the page copy or offer needs work.
**What it tells you:** Is the headline compelling? Does the offer feel worth an email address?

---

### Metric 2: Email Open Rate
**What it is:** Of everyone on your list, what % opens each email?
**How to track:** Built into every email tool (Mailchimp, Kit, etc.)
**What's good:** 30–50% for a warm audience. Real estate and local audiences typically run high.
**What it tells you:** Are your subject lines working? Is the audience engaged?

---

### Metric 3: Click-Through Rate (CTR)
**What it is:** Of people who open your email, what % clicks a link (to book a call, visit the site, etc.)?
**How to track:** Built into your email tool
**What's good:** 3–8% on follow-up emails. Higher means the CTA is landing.
**What it tells you:** Are people interested enough to take action?

---

### Metric 4: Cost Per Lead (CPL) — If Running Ads
**What it is:** Total ad spend ÷ number of email signups
**How to track:** Facebook Ads Manager shows you this automatically
**What's good:** For real estate, $5–$20 CPL is reasonable. Over $30 means the ad or landing page needs work.
**What it tells you:** Is paid distribution worth continuing?

---

### Metric 5: Lead-to-Conversation Rate
**What it is:** Of all the leads generated, how many turned into an actual call or email conversation?
**How to track:** Manually at first — just count the booked calls that came from people who found you through the guide
**What's good:** Even 5–10% is meaningful in real estate given transaction size
**What it tells you:** Is the follow-up sequence working? Is the CTA compelling?

---

### Metric 6: Closed Transactions Attributed to Lead Magnet
**What it is:** How many actual home purchases can be traced back to this lead magnet?
**How to track:** Ask every new client "how did you find us?" — track in a simple spreadsheet or CRM (Follow Up Boss is popular in real estate)
**What's good:** Even 1–2 transactions in the first 3 months covers the cost of everything listed here many times over
**What it tells you:** The ultimate ROI metric

## When to Declare It Working

**Green lights:**
- Landing page converting at 20%+
- Email open rates above 30%
- At least 1 inbound conversation/call attributed to the guide within 30 days
- Growing list week over week without paid ads

**Red flags that need attention:**
- Lots of landing page traffic but low signups → Headline or offer isn't resonating
- Good signups but nobody opens emails → Subject lines need work, or wrong audience
- Good opens but nobody books a call → Follow-up sequence too pushy OR not clear enough on next step
- Lots of calls but no transactions → Qualification issue — the leads may not be ready to buy

## 90-Day Timeline

**Week 1–2:** Write content, design PDF, build landing page, set up email tool and automation
**Week 3:** Soft launch — share with existing contacts and on socials organically. Gather first data.
**Week 4:** Review metrics, adjust landing page copy if needed, write follow-up email sequence
**Month 2:** Full social push, pitch to Facebook groups, add to blog posts
**Month 3:** Evaluate paid ads if organic is working. Consider a second lead magnet for sellers.
